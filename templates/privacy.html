Privacy Policy

This privacy policy has been created to inform users about the data collection, usage, and protection practices of our website and Discord bot. This privacy policy does not apply to any third-party services or websites that may be linked to or integrate with our service, and we are not responsible for the privacy practices of these third parties.

Discord-Related Information

This section covers the Discord-related information that's stored by Tornium and Tornium bot.

  • User Discord ID: The Discord IDs of users who (directly and indirectly) interact with Tornium's services. This ID is used to reference the Discord user's Torn account.
  • Server IDs and Names: The Discord IDs of servers the bot is in.

Log Files

This service follows a standard procedure of using log files on user usage on the service. The information collected by these log files can include IP adddresses, brower information, timestamps, referrers, and more. This information is for error monitoring and debugging, and this information will not be used for analytics.

Datadog

Datadog provides real-time error logging for the service so we can resolve issues. If an action causes an error, certain user data may be sent to Datadog to help reproduce the issuue. user data may include your account information, the interaction that's occured, and other data. This data is deleted after 30 days, and is subject to Datadog's privacy policy.

Security

We take reasonable steps to protect the information provided via the services from loss, misuse, and unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or distruction. However, no internet transmission is ever fully secure of error free.

By contributing to Tornium or Tornium Bot, you agree to the developer certification of origin. This certifies that you have written the code or have the right to submit the code to the project.
